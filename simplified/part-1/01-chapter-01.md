## God and the Infinite

<p id="1-1-1" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.1</span> God is the Supreme Intelligence, the First Cause of all things.</p>

<p id="1-1-2" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.2</span> God is not merely a force or impersonal principle, but the highest intelligence and the source of everything.</p>

<p id="1-1-3" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.3</span> The infinite is what has no beginning, end, or limit. It can also refer to what human understanding cannot fully grasp.</p>

<p id="1-1-4" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.4</span> For this reason, it is not enough to say only that God is the Infinite.</p>

<p id="1-1-5" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.5</span> That expression is incomplete. God is infinite in perfection, but the infinite by itself is an abstract idea, not a full description of God. It points to God's greatness, but does not express all that God is as the Supreme Intelligence and First Cause of all things.</p>

## Proofs of the Existence of God

<p id="1-1-6" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.6</span> There is no effect without a cause. Since the universe exists, it must have a cause. To deny God, one would have to deny this basic principle and say that something can come from nothing.</p>

<p id="1-1-7" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.7</span> Human beings also carry an inner sense that God exists. This feeling is too widespread to be explained only by teaching. Even where formal instruction is lacking, the idea of a Supreme Being appears again and again.</p>

<p id="1-1-8" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.8</span> Some claim that matter itself is the first cause. But matter and its properties also need a cause. So matter cannot be the ultimate source of everything.</p>

<p id="1-1-9" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.9</span> Others explain the universe by chance. But chance is blind. It cannot account for the order, balance, and harmony seen in creation. The laws of nature point to intelligence.</p>

<p id="1-1-10" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.10</span> We know the first cause by its works. A work shows something about the one who made it. The greatness of the universe, and the wisdom seen in its order, point to an intelligence far above humanity.</p>

<p id="1-1-11" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.11</span> Whatever name is used, that supreme source is the first cause of all things: a Supreme Intelligence above every other intelligence.</p>

## The Attributes of the Divinity

<p id="1-1-12" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.12</span> Human beings cannot fully understand the inner nature of God.</p>

<p id="1-1-13" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.13</span> Our present abilities are limited while the spirit is still influenced by matter. As the spirit becomes purified, it can approach God more clearly, though never completely.</p>

<p id="1-1-14" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.14</span> Even without grasping God’s essence, reason can recognize certain necessary divine perfections. When God is called eternal, immutable, immaterial, one, all-powerful, and supremely just and good, this gives a true human idea, though not full knowledge. If any of these qualities were lacking, God would not be supreme.</p>

### Eternal

<p id="1-1-15" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.15</span> God is eternal.</p>

<p id="1-1-16" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.16</span> If God had a beginning, God would have come either from nothing or from some prior being. Neither is possible.</p>

### Immutable

<p id="1-1-17" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.17</span> God is immutable.</p>

<p id="1-1-18" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.18</span> If God changed, the laws of the universe would not be stable. The order of creation requires a foundation that does not vary.</p>

### Immaterial

<p id="1-1-19" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.19</span> God is immaterial.</p>

<p id="1-1-20" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.20</span> If God were material, God would be subject to change, and so would not be immutable.</p>

### One

<p id="1-1-21" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.21</span> God is one.</p>

<p id="1-1-22" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.22</span> If there were many gods, there could not be one plan or one supreme power in the universe.</p>

### All-Powerful

<p id="1-1-23" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.23</span> God is all-powerful because God is one.</p>

<p id="1-1-24" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.24</span> If God were not supreme in power, something else would be equal or greater.</p>

### Supremely Just and Good

<p id="1-1-25" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.25</span> God is supremely just and good.</p>

<p id="1-1-26" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.26</span> The laws of existence show divine wisdom, justice, and goodness, which in creation are inseparable.</p>

## Pantheism

<p id="1-1-27" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.27</span> God is not the universe itself, nor the total of all beings and worlds.</p>

<p id="1-1-28" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.28</span> If God were only the sum of created things, then God would be an effect rather than the first cause. God cannot be both cause and effect. The idea of God requires a being who exists by himself, not one produced by the universe.</p>

<p id="1-1-29" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.29</span> Pantheism teaches that everything is God, and God is everything. This can seem attractive because the universe is immense, ordered, and intelligent. But it confuses the Creator with creation.</p>

<p id="1-1-30" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.30</span> If God were identical with matter or with changing beings, then God would also change. But matter and created things are variable, dependent, and limited. So these properties cannot be assigned to God.</p>

### Creator and Creation

<p id="1-1-31" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.31</span> The difference between God and the universe is not just one of size or power. God belongs to a different order.</p>

<p id="1-1-32" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.32</span> A machine is not the engineer who designed it. A painting is not the artist. In the same way, creation shows divine intelligence, wisdom, and power, but it is not God.</p>

<p id="1-1-33" className="verse"><span className="verse-num">1.1.33</span> Pantheism fails because it removes this distinction. By making the universe identical with God, it weakens the divine attributes and denies the full independence of the Creator.</p>
