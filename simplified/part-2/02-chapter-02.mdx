## Incarnation

<SectionImage src="/img/2.2.1-2.2.6.webp" alt="Incarnation" />


<p id="2-2-1" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-1" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.1"><span className="verse-num">2.2.1</span></a> Incarnation is given to spirits so they can move toward perfection. For some it is expiation; for others, a mission. Through the conditions, struggles, and trials of bodily life, spirits are taught, corrected, and refined.</p>

<p id="2-2-2" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-2" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.2"><span className="verse-num">2.2.2</span></a> Embodied life also places spirits within the work of creation. On each world, a spirit receives a body suited to that world and helps carry out the divine order proper to its condition, while advancing itself.</p>

<p id="2-2-3" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-3" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.3"><span className="verse-num">2.2.3</span></a> Thus bodily activity is necessary to creation and also serves as a means of progress, drawing spirits nearer to God. By this law, all things are connected in mutual solidarity.</p>

### Is Incarnation Necessary for Spirits Who Have Followed the Path of Good from the Beginning?

<p id="2-2-4" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-4" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.4"><span className="verse-num">2.2.4</span></a> Incarnation is necessary for all spirits. All are created simple and ignorant, and gain instruction through the trials of embodied life. Divine justice does not grant happiness without merit.</p>

<p id="2-2-5" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-5" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.5"><span className="verse-num">2.2.5</span></a> A spirit that follows the good is not freed from bodily life, but reaches the goal more quickly. As a spirit becomes purer, it has fewer sufferings to endure, since many pains come from moral faults.</p>

<p id="2-2-6" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-6" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.6"><span className="verse-num">2.2.6</span></a> Incarnation is therefore not only a punishment or burden. It is the means by which spirits learn, make repair, serve, and progress, while taking part in the order of creation.</p>

## The Soul

<SectionImage src="/img/2.2.7-2.2.31.webp" alt="The Soul" />


<p id="2-2-7" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-7" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.7"><span className="verse-num">2.2.7</span></a> The soul is an incarnate spirit.</p>

<p id="2-2-8" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-8" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.8"><span className="verse-num">2.2.8</span></a> Before joining a body, it is called a spirit. During bodily life, it is called a soul. They are not two different beings, but the same being in two states.</p>

### The Three Elements in Human Beings

<p id="2-2-9" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-9" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.9"><span className="verse-num">2.2.9</span></a> Human beings are made up of three essential parts.</p>

#### 1) The Body

<p id="2-2-10" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-10" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.10"><span className="verse-num">2.2.10</span></a> The body is the material part. In its organic nature it is like that of animals and is animated by the same vital principle. By itself, it is only an outer covering.</p>

#### 2) The Soul

<p id="2-2-11" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-11" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.11"><span className="verse-num">2.2.11</span></a> The soul is the incarnate spirit, the intelligent and moral being that lives in the body.</p>

#### 3) The Perispirit

<p id="2-2-12" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-12" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.12"><span className="verse-num">2.2.12</span></a> The perispirit is the intermediate element joining soul and body. It is semi-material and makes communication between them possible.</p>

### The Bond Between Soul and Body

<p id="2-2-13" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-13" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.13"><span className="verse-num">2.2.13</span></a> Soul and body are joined by a link between matter and spirit.</p>

<p id="2-2-14" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-14" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.14"><span className="verse-num">2.2.14</span></a> The body can exist without the soul, but only as living matter without intelligence. The soul cannot live in a body that has no organic life. The union is completed at birth and lasts until death breaks the bond.</p>

<p id="2-2-15" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-15" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.15"><span className="verse-num">2.2.15</span></a> One spirit cannot be incarnated in two bodies at the same time, because spirit is indivisible.</p>

### The Soul and the Vital Principle

<p id="2-2-16" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-16" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.16"><span className="verse-num">2.2.16</span></a> The soul must not be confused with the principle of material life.</p>

<p id="2-2-17" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-17" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.17"><span className="verse-num">2.2.17</span></a> Some use the word soul to mean the force that animates living beings. But the soul, properly speaking, is a distinct moral being, independent of matter and keeping its individuality. Many disputes come from using the same word for different ideas.</p>

### The Soul Is Indivisible

<p id="2-2-18" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-18" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.18"><span className="verse-num">2.2.18</span></a> The soul is not divided among the organs or muscles.</p>

<p id="2-2-19" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-19" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.19"><span className="verse-num">2.2.19</span></a> That idea only applies if soul means the vital fluid spread through the body. If it means the incarnate spirit, the soul is one and indivisible, acting on the organs through the intermediate fluid that animates them.</p>

### The Soul and Its Envelopes

<p id="2-2-20" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-20" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.20"><span className="verse-num">2.2.20</span></a> The soul is not shut inside the body like a bird in a cage.</p>

<p id="2-2-21" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-21" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.21"><span className="verse-num">2.2.21</span></a> It radiates beyond the body. It is surrounded by two envelopes: first the perispirit, then the material body.</p>

### The Soul in Childhood and Adulthood

<p id="2-2-22" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-22" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.22"><span className="verse-num">2.2.22</span></a> The spirit in a child is no less complete than in an adult.</p>

<p id="2-2-23" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-23" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.23"><span className="verse-num">2.2.23</span></a> What develops is not the soul itself, but the bodily organs through which it expresses itself.</p>

### Why Spirits Speak Differently About the Soul

<p id="2-2-24" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-24" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.24"><span className="verse-num">2.2.24</span></a> Spirits do not all speak of the soul in the same way because they are not equally advanced.</p>

<p id="2-2-25" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-25" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.25"><span className="verse-num">2.2.25</span></a> Some understand little, some only seem learned, and even enlightened spirits may use different words for the same reality because human language is limited. Figurative language is often mistaken for literal teaching.</p>

### The World Soul

<p id="2-2-26" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-26" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.26"><span className="verse-num">2.2.26</span></a> The expression world soul can mean the universal principle of life and intelligence from which individual beings come.</p>

<p id="2-2-27" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-27" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.27"><span className="verse-num">2.2.27</span></a> It is often vague. In a better sense, it can also mean the gathering of devoted spirits who help guide human actions toward the good.</p>

### On Philosophical Disputes About the Soul

<p id="2-2-28" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-28" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.28"><span className="verse-num">2.2.28</span></a> Philosophical disagreements about the soul do not make the search useless.</p>

<p id="2-2-29" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-29" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.29"><span className="verse-num">2.2.29</span></a> Even mistaken systems helped prepare the way for clearer understanding, since truth and error were often mixed together.</p>

### The Seat of the Soul

<p id="2-2-30" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-30" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.30"><span className="verse-num">2.2.30</span></a> The soul does not occupy one exact point in the body.</p>

<p id="2-2-31" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-31" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.31"><span className="verse-num">2.2.31</span></a> It may be said to reside especially where certain faculties are most active, such as the head for thought or the heart for feeling. But this is not anatomical. The soul remains the conscious, indivisible spirit, united to the body by the perispirit while remaining distinct from it.</p>

## Materialism

<SectionImage src="/img/2.2.32-2.2.41.webp" alt="Materialism" />


### Materialism

<p id="2-2-32" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-32" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.32"><span className="verse-num">2.2.32</span></a> Those who study the natural sciences may be drawn toward materialism when they judge only by what they can see. If observation is limited to visible mechanisms, it is easy to mistake the instrument for the whole reality. Pride can reinforce this, leading people to deny what science cannot yet measure.</p>

<p id="2-2-33" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-33" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.33"><span className="verse-num">2.2.33</span></a> But materialism is not the natural result of science. It comes from misusing science. Some who claim to believe in nothingness are less certain than they appear, and often accept hope when it is offered.</p>

<p id="2-2-34" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-34" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.34"><span className="verse-num">2.2.34</span></a> Materialism reduces intelligence to matter alone. It treats the body as a machine and life as the work of organs. Because the soul cannot be grasped by physical tools, it concludes that thought depends only on matter and that after death everything ends.</p>

<p id="2-2-35" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-35" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.35"><span className="verse-num">2.2.35</span></a> If that were true, the moral consequences would be grave. Good and evil would lose higher meaning, people would live only for themselves and pleasure, and social bonds would weaken. A society built on such a belief would carry the cause of its own ruin.</p>

<p id="2-2-36" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-36" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.36"><span className="verse-num">2.2.36</span></a> Fortunately, this view is not general. The human heart resists it. Whatever people may argue, when death approaches most still ask what will become of them.</p>

<p id="2-2-37" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-37" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.37"><span className="verse-num">2.2.37</span></a> It is hard to accept absolute nothingness: the loss of every faculty, affection, and hope, and eternal separation from those we love. Religion denies this, and reason supports that denial. Still, many want more than a vague future.</p>

<p id="2-2-38" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-38" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.38"><span className="verse-num">2.2.38</span></a> It is one thing to say the soul exists and another to understand what that means. People ask whether it keeps its individuality, what future happiness or suffering is, and how that life is experienced. They want something that speaks to reason and to the heart.</p>

<p id="2-2-39" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-39" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.39"><span className="verse-num">2.2.39</span></a> It is false to say that no one has returned to speak of the life beyond. Through spirit communications, the future life is shown as a reality made known through facts. Spirits describe their condition, occupations, and new existence, so the unseen world is no longer only a theory.</p>

<p id="2-2-40" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-40" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.40"><span className="verse-num">2.2.40</span></a> Thus each soul’s destiny appears as the natural result of its merits and faults. This does not weaken Christian faith but supports it, restoring belief to doubters and confidence to the hesitant.</p>

<p id="2-2-41" className="verse"><a href="#2-2-41" className="verse-link" aria-label="Link to verse 2.2.41"><span className="verse-num">2.2.41</span></a> For that reason, the revelation of the future life strongly supports religion. It renews hope, steadies uncertain hearts, and helps lead people back to goodness by giving them a clearer sense of what awaits them.</p>
